Tt DPK Global Law and Development Fellowship Program

A limited fellowship season is now open. Tetra Tech DPK welcomes applications from candidates interested in a funded fellowship opportunity in Liberia (starting in January 2017 for 5-6 months) or who can self-fund fellowship postings in 2017 to our other projects that express a need. Self-funding applicants must be willing to be posted to whichever of our projects are most in need of Global Fellows. Applications will be accepted on a rolling basis. Please clearly indicate in your application materials whether you are applying for a Liberia fellowship or a self-funded fellowship or both, and your dates of availability.

Tetra Tech DPK’s Global Law and Development Fellowship Program provides a short-term concentrated experience for students enrolled in, or professionals having completed, graduate programs in law, international development, court administration, public policy, or related fields. The program provides exposure to the management and implementation of international development projects on access to justice, anti-corruption, or various aspects of the rule of law. Fellows spend 8 to 13 weeks assigned to one of our field projects.

RESPONSIBILITIES OF TT DPK GLOBAL LAW AND DEVELOPMENT FELLOWS

Responsibilities of Tt DPK’s Global Law and Development fellows typically include some or all of the following:

      • Completing a specific technical work product
      • Providing technical support to achieve contract deliverables
      • Collecting and analyzing project reports
      • Identifying and capturing best practices and success stories
      • Proofreading and editing reports and documents
      • Organizing training courses and events
      • Assisting with international consultant briefings
      • Providing administrative support for programmatic activities
      • Keeping notes of trainings and meetings
      • Preparing a final report on experiences and lesson learned

PLACEMENTS OF TT DPK GLOBAL LAW AND DEVELOPMENT FELLOWS

Since the inauguration of the fellowship program in 2010, fellows have served with distinction in our Afghanistan, Côte d'Ivoire, Democratic Republic of Congo, Georgia, Haiti, Iraq, Jamaica, Jordan, and Liberia projects. Each year the Tt DPK fellowship committee identifies which projects would benefit from hosting a fellow, and selects fellowship finalists with relevant language skills who have successfully completed the application and interview process. The 2017 fellowship season is limited to fellowship postings only for Liberia (for a funded fellowship) or for candidates who can self-fund to other projects. Applicants must be willing to be posted to whichever of our projects are most in need of Global Fellows. French- and Spanish-speaking applicants are needed for at least one posting each.

HOW TO APPLY FOR THE FELLOWSHIP PROGRAM

To apply for the Tetra Tech DPK Global Law and Development Fellowship Program, please submit the following application materials to [Click to Reveal Address] referencing 2017 fellowship program in the subject line:

      • Cover letter explaining your interest in participating in the fellowship program and clearly stating your period(s) of availability. Fellowships are awarded year-round. The cover letter should also indicate your interest in a funded Liberia fellowship or a self-funded fellowship elsewhere, and any languages you speak (French and Spanish will be needed for at least one posting in 2017).
      • Resume clearly indicating any periods of overseas work or study.
      • Contact information for three professional or academic references
      • Writing sample in English (five pages or less)
      • For Liberia fellowship applicants, a seperate statement, consisting of one paragraph outlining how past experience confirms your suitability for a rural posting in Africa with frequent travels in rough positions, and a second paragraph indicating suitability and experience on monitoring and evaluation/data collection

CONTACT INFORMATION ABOUT THE FELLOWSHIP PROGRAM

Due to the high volume of applicants, please no phone calls. Short-listed applicants will be contacted directly.
