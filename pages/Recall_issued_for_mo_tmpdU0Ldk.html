Recall issued for more than 1.9 million pounds of ready-to-eat chicken products

Recall issued for more than 1.9 million pounds of ready-to-eat chicken products

Some of the products may have been undercooked

Some of the products may have been undercooked

Selima HussainContact Reporter
A massive recall for precooked chicken products has been issued by the U.S. Department of Agriculture.
Privacy Policy

Nearly 2 million pounds of ready-to-eat chicken products from National Steak and Poultry are being recalled after some of the products appeared to be undercooked, the U.S. Department of Agriculture’s Food Safety and Inspection Service announced Sunday.

The department said the possible undercooking could lead to the survival of bacteria in the products, presenting a severe health hazard for consumers. They classified the health risk as “Class 1,” which means “there is a reasonable probability that the use of this product will cause serious, adverse health consequences or death,” according to a statement. 

No reports of illness or adverse side effects from consuming the recalled products have been released.  

The massive recall affects certain products produced from Aug. 20 to Nov. 30. These products -- ranging from 5 lb. bags of boneless chicken breasts to chicken breast strips -- have the number “P-6010T” inside the USDA mark of inspection. For a full list of products, click here. The department said a full list of retail locations will be posted online when it becomes available. 

A previous recall that affected 17,439 pounds of product was issued on Nov. 23. The recall was expanded to more than 1.9 million after a consumer complained that that the product looked undercooked.
